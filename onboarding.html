Interoception Study

Participant Onboarding

This session will walk you through study setup, consent, scheduling, and a device check. It takes about 10 minutes.

Study Overview

This is a 2-week study investigating interoceptive awareness — your ability to perceive internal body signals like your heartbeat.

Phase Adjustment Task (PAT)

Once during this onboarding session, you will complete a task where you match an auditory tone to your heartbeat using a dial. Your phone's camera measures your pulse optically through your fingertip. This task is done in a controlled setting.

Daily Check-Ins (EMA)

3 times per day for 14 days, you'll receive a link to a brief check-in (~1 min). You'll rate your body awareness, stress, and describe any physical sensations. Timing is flexible within windows you choose next.

What You'll Need

A smartphone with a rear camera and flashlight. Wired earphones or a quiet room (no Bluetooth headphones — the latency invalidates timing data).

Informed Consent

↓ Scroll to read the full document

1. Purpose of the Study

This research investigates individual differences in interoceptive accuracy — the ability to detect internal physiological signals such as heartbeats. You are being asked to participate because you responded to our recruitment materials and meet the eligibility criteria.

2. Procedures

If you agree to participate, you will: (a) complete an in-lab Phase Adjustment Task lasting approximately 25 minutes, during which your phone's camera will measure your pulse through your fingertip while you match auditory tones to your heartbeat; (b) complete brief Ecological Momentary Assessments (EMAs) three times daily for 14 consecutive days, delivered via personalized URL links.

3. Risks and Discomforts

Risks are minimal. The camera-based pulse measurement requires holding your finger over the phone's camera and flashlight, which may cause mild warmth. Some participants may experience mild anxiety when attending to their heartbeat. You may stop at any time.

4. Benefits

There are no direct benefits to you. This research may contribute to understanding interoceptive processing and its relationship to emotional regulation and wellbeing.

5. Compensation

You will receive [COMPENSATION DETAILS] for completing the study. Partial compensation is available for partial completion as described in the recruitment materials.

6. Confidentiality

Your data will be identified only by your participant ID number. No personally identifying information is collected by the task application. All data is stored on encrypted, access-controlled servers. De-identified data may be shared in publications or with other researchers.

7. Voluntary Participation

Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time without penalty by contacting the research team. Withdrawal does not affect your relationship with the university.

8. Contact Information

For questions about this study, contact [PI NAME] at [EMAIL]. For questions about your rights as a participant, contact the Institutional Review Board at [IRB CONTACT].

9. Data Collection Details

The Phase Adjustment Task records: optical pulse waveform data (brightness values from camera), computed heart rate, tone timing adjustments, confidence ratings, and body sensation locations. The EMA records: subjective ratings of body awareness and stress, and free-text descriptions of physical sensations. No audio, video, or images of your person are recorded or transmitted.

1. Purpose

This research investigates individual differences in how people perceive internal body signals such as heartbeats, and how this relates to mood and daily experience.

2. What You Will Do

You will complete brief daily check-ins (about 1 minute each, 3 times per day) for the duration of the study. During in-lab visits, you will complete a task where you adjust an auditory tone to match your heartbeat using a dial. Your phone's rear camera measures your pulse optically through your fingertip using the flashlight. No audio, video, or images of your person are recorded.

3. Risks

Risks are minimal. You may experience mild boredom or fatigue. The camera-based heart rate measurement is non-invasive and poses no physical risk.

4. Confidentiality

All data are stored under a participant ID number with no identifying information. Data are encrypted in transit and stored on secure servers. Your data will not be shared with anyone outside the research team.

5. Voluntary Participation

Participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time without penalty. Withdrawing will not affect your relationship with the university or any compensation already earned.

6. Compensation

Compensation details were provided in the recruitment materials. Partial compensation is available for partial completion per the schedule provided by your researcher.

7. Contact

For questions about this study, contact the Affective Science Lab at OSU. For questions about your rights as a research participant, contact the OSU Institutional Review Board.

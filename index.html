Phase Adjustment Task

Cardiac Interoception Protocol

Audio Setup

This experimental task requires precision timing to match an auditory stimulus with your heartbeat.

⚠️ Crucial constraint: Do not use Bluetooth headphones.

Wireless headphones introduce latency that invalidates phase measurement.

Please use wired earphones or the built-in device speaker in a quiet environment.

Sensor Preparation

Place the tip of your index finger gently over both the rear camera lens and the flashlight.

Keep your finger perfectly still. The system uses photoplethysmography (PPG) to record your pulse; movement artifacts will disrupt data collection.

Calibration Complete

In the main task, you will hear a series of auditory tones. Each tone represents one cardiac cycle, but its timing may be artificially shifted.

Your objective is to rotate the measurement dial until the tone feels perfectly in phase with your actual heartbeat.

Task Constraints

Do not actively palpate your pulse with your free hand. We are measuring internal interoceptive perception.

Maintain steady finger contact with the optical sensor throughout the entire block.

You will complete 2 practice trials before the main protocol begins.

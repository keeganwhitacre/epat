Phase Adjustment Task

Cardiac Interoception

How well can you match a sound with your heartbeat?

Let's find out!

Find a quiet place where you can sit comfortably with earphones for about 10 minutes.

We'll begin by capturing a couple of minutes of your heart rate to get a baseline reading.

Getting ready

Place the tip of your index finger over both the camera and flashlight on the back of your phone.

Keep your finger still — even small movements can disrupt the reading.

The flashlight will turn on so the camera can detect your pulse through your fingertip.

Baseline complete

In the main task, you'll hear a series of tones. Each tone represents one of your heartbeats — but it may be delayed or early.

Your job is to rotate a dial until the tone feels in sync with your actual heartbeat.

After each trial, you'll rate your confidence. Every 4th trial, you'll mark where on your body you felt your heartbeat.

Important

Do not actively try to feel your pulse with your hand — we're only interested in what you naturally feel.

Keep your finger on the camera throughout each trial.

You'll start with 2 practice trials, then 20 real trials.

Sit comfortably upright with your earphones on and press "Start" when ready.
